About Me

I am currently a senior at Georgetown University in Washington, D.C., majoring in linguistics and minoring in Turkish.

My main interests include language documentation, phonology and typology. So far, my work has centered on West Africa: I've done fieldwork in Côte d'Ivoire and have worked (to various extents) on Guébie [Kru, Côte d'Ivoire], Mòoré [Gur, Burkina Faso], Gã [Kwa, Ghana] and Ébrié [Kwa, Côte d'Ivoire].

Research Experience

Fieldwork: Documenting Guébie 2019 - ongoing

I did fieldwork in Gnagbodougnoa, Côte d'Ivoire in summer 2019, documenting Guébie, an endangered Kru language. That fieldwork involved conducting elicitation sessions, recording storytelling, and running experiments. Since returning to the US, I've continued to analyze the data that was collected.

Fieldwork: Describing Mòoré 2018 - ongoing

I have conducted elicitation sessions with two native speaker consultants of Mòoré, a Gur language spoken in Burkina Faso. I've used the data collected in a brief typological description of the language, and continue to work on an analysis of the tone system.
